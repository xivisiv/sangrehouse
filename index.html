THE METHOD TO MADNESS

“And those who were seen dancing were thought to be insane by those who could not hear the music.” — Nietzsche

The earliest musicians built relationships with natural sounds in the environment. Be it by hitting an object by accident and hearing its resonance echo, the rhythmic beating of walking, or the sound of firewood igniting.

The journey begins with Active Listening.

The continuance of this cycle builds the Inner Instrument which hears and processes this relationship as music. This inner instrument is the creative principle which is inherent to all of the arts. As the listener becomes trained, the sound-waves transform into emotions, ideas, and states of experience.

This ongoing relationship, paired with body responsiveness, leads to the third and final act: Intentionality.

Inner Music

Inner Music trains this original connection.

It teaches the body to hear and the ear to feel.

Every exercise begins with presence and awareness, not technique. You learn to move as sound moves, to respond as nature responds — naturally, precisely, without hesitation.

As your perception sharpens, the music starts to shape itself. Theory and structure emerge as reflections of what you already sense.

AIM: Tuning the Instrument

My aim is to fully equip students with the necessary building blocks to connect themself to the music inside sound — through whichever instrument of their choosing. Through the tuning of the body to music, the client’s personal relationship to music will be established. And as the sessions progress, we will explore more complex and intricate aspects of music and artistry as they become necessary.

Foundational Practice

  • Listening — Developing sensitivity to tone, space, rhythm, and silence.
  • Resonance — Feeling vibration in the body and learning to respond through movement.
  • Expression — Exploring how awareness becomes sound through your chosen instrument.
  • Reflection — Refining your aesthetic judgment by noticing what feels aligned, what feels false, what asks to return.

What To Expect

  • Refined aesthetic taste (become aware of the choices you can make and how to achieve them).
  • Knowledge of music theory (as needed—assessed based on student’s needs).
  • Connection between creativity and intention so that each time you play music you can be inspired.
  • The ability to play songs of your liking.
  • Ongoing support with technical issues relevant to playing, singing, creating, and/or producing music.

The Meeting Point: Physics & Psychology

Physics of Sound

The harmonic overtone series is the foundational meeting point between psychology and physics. Musical notes are tuned to the natural resonance frequency responses apparent all around in nature.

The instrument is one piece of the puzzle. The way it’s played, the emotional state of the player, the room it resonates in, and how the musician is listening to the sound echoing back all create an intricate relationship that gives rise to the creative principle.

This won’t be a course on the math, but an overview of the fundamental principles that are relevant for a musician to know.

The Art of Friction

Every artist must become well-versed in the art of FRICTION.

No authentic and self-expressive work of art was written without some sort of friction building the gravity for it.

With Inner Music, we build sufficient understanding of how to direct the frustration of learning and creating toward the goal of self-expression and artistry.

ACT I

ACTIVE LISTENING

The earliest musicians built relationships with natural sounds in the environment. Be it by hitting an object by accident and hearing its resonance echo, the rhythmic beating of walking, or the sound of firewood igniting.

The first step is Active Listening.

This develops an awareness of the relationship between mind and environment.

The active listener develops the ability to respond, in their body, to the music outside and inside. This is done by training the body to follow the gravity of rhythm as well as the ability to follow the sequences which create notes, scales, and chords.

ACT II

INTERNAL INSTRUMENT

The continuance of this cycle (Active Listening $\to$ Body Response) builds the internal “instrument” which hears and processes this relationship as music. This inner instrument is the creative principle which is inherent to all of the arts, specifically music.

Through active listening, selective hearing that is directed by your internal emotional state begins to form a third awareness - the inner instrument.

Sounds begin to seem as if they are resonating against your emotions. An implication of directionality is developed and access to states of flow become available.

A learned listener of sound, by sheer act of listening, internally resonates to the atmosphere of sound like an instrument.

ACT III

INTENTIONALITY

The combination of Active Listening and body responsiveness through the Inner Instrument creates the ability to respond to sound with intentionality. With this ability to be intentional, we can begin to explore aesthetic choices or more complex music theoretical constructs which fine tune and expand the relationships we can have with sound to build the direct and authentic experience we are called upon.

As the active listener becomes trained, the spectrum of sound transforms from a mere combination sound-waves to something like emotions, ideas, and states of experience.

In this world, all musical relationships are in a state of flux and creativity is linked to vital and raw unconscious processes.
