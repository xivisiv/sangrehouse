A MEETING POINT BETWEEN PSYCHOLOGY AND THE PHYSICS OF SOUND

Physics of Sound

The harmonic overtone series is the foundational meeting point between psychology and physics. Musical notes are tuned to the natural resonance frequency responses apparent all around in nature.

Every sound echoes to musical principles that connect to various human emotions.

The instrument is one piece of the puzzle… The way it’s played, the emotional state of the player, the room it resonates in, and how the musician is listening all create an intricate relationship that gives rise to the creative principle.

*If you wish to hear more about the physics of sound in your session, let me know. This won’t be a course on the math, but an overview of the fundamental principles.*

Friction

Every artist must become well-versed in the art of FRICTION.

No authentic and self-expressive work of art was written without some sort of friction building the gravity for it.

With Inner Music, we build sufficient understanding of how to direct the frustration of learning and creating toward the goal of self-expression and artistry.

This approach transforms resistance into a creative force, teaching you to use challenge as the source code for genuine, compelling art.

Preparation Checklist *:

ACT I

ACTIVE LISTENING

The first step is Active Listening.

This develops an awareness of the relationship between mind and environment.

The active listener develops the ability to respond, in their body, to the music outside and inside. This is done by training the body to follow the gravity of rhythm as well as the ability to follow the sequences which create notes, scales, and chords.

ACT II

INTERNAL INSTRUMENT

The continuance of this cycle builds the internal “instrument” which hears and processes this relationship as music. This inner instrument is the creative principle which is inherent to all of the arts, specifically music.

Through active listening, selective hearing that is directed by your internal emotional state begins to form a third awareness - the inner instrument. Sounds begin to seem as if they are resonating against your emotions. An implication of directionality is developed and access to states of flow become available.

A learned listener of sound, by sheer act of listening, internally resonates to the atmosphere of sound like an instrument.

ACT III

INTENTIONALITY

The combination of Active Listening and body responsiveness through the Inner Instrument creates the ability to respond to sound with intentionality. With this ability to be intentional, we can begin to explore aesthetic choices or more complex music theoretical constructs which fine tune and expand the relationships we can have with sound to build the direct and authentic experience we are called upon.

As the active listener becomes trained, the spectrum of sound transforms from a mere combination sound-waves to something like emotions, ideas, and states of experience.

In this world, all musical relationships are in a state of flux and creativity is linked to vital and raw unconscious processes.
