The Founding Principle

“And those who were seen dancing were thought to be insane by those who could not hear the music.” — Nietzsche

The first musicians built a **relationship with a sound** they heard in nature. Be it by hitting something by accident and hearing its resonance echo, or by hearing the rhythmic beating of a motion like walking.

Active listening is the first step.

The continuance of this cycle builds the instrument that creates the relationship between listener and object that we call music. This instrument is the **creative principle** of music which is inherent to all beings.

ACT I - LISTENER × ACT II - RESPONSE × ACT III - INTENTION

About the Inner Music

Inner Music trains this **original connection**.

It teaches the **body to hear** and the **ear to feel**.

Every exercise begins with **presence and awareness**, not technique.

You learn to move as sound moves, to respond as nature responds — naturally, precisely, without hesitation.

As your perception sharpens, the music starts to shape itself. Theory and structure emerge as reflections of what you already sense.

The Aim

To tune the human instrument.

To restore the physical and emotional intelligence that gives rise to music.

To make creation an act of will.

Latest Practices and Gains

  • Resonance: Feeling vibration in the body and learning to respond through movement.
  • Listening: Developing sensitivity to tone, space, rhythm, and silence.
  • Expression: Exploring how awareness becomes sound through your chosen instrument.
  • Reflection: Refining your aesthetic judgment.

What to Expect

  • Growth of ear and musical perception.
  • Cultivation of taste and creative instinct.
  • Theoretical understanding as it becomes relevant.
  • The ability to play songs of your liking.

Friction: The Artist's Gravity

Every artist must become well-versed in the art of FRICTION.

No authentic and self-expressive work of art was written without some sort of friction building the gravity for it.

With Inner Music, we build sufficient understanding of how to direct the frustration of learning and creating toward the goal of self-expression and artistry.

The **harmonic overtone series** is the foundational meeting point between psychology and physics. Every sound echoes to musical principles that connect to various human emotions.

The way an instrument is played, the **emotional state of the player**, and how the musician listens to the sound create an intricate relationship that gives rise to the creative principle.
